M-DAFTO: Multi-Stage Deferred Acceptance Based
Fair Task Of∩¼éoading in IoT-Fog Systems
Chittaranjan Swain , Graduate Student Member , IEEE, Manmath Narayan Sahoo , Member , IEEE,

AbstractΓÇöResource-constrained Internet of Things (IoT) de-
vices depend on remote Cloud/Fog Nodes (FNs) to execute deadline-
sensitive services. Of∩¼éoading computations of real-time services
to a remote cloud server results in intolerable latency due to
intermittent channels, higher transmission delays, and scarce spec-
trum resources. Therefore, of∩¼éoading to nearby FNs is preferable;
however, it introduces several signi∩¼ücant issues: ( i) allocation of
limited FN resources, ( ii) deadline constraint of heterogeneous
services, and (iii) requirement of computationally inexpensive and
scalable strategies. This article proposes a M-DAFTO model to
tackle the abovementioned issues and generate a fair of∩¼éoading
plan in polynomial time. The of∩¼éoading problem is modeled as a
many-to-one matching game with maximum and minimum quotas
at each FN. Because the deferred acceptance (DA) algorithm fails
to operate with minimum quotas, we adopt a variant of the DA
algorithm, a multistage deferred acceptance (MSDA) algorithm, to
solve the of∩¼éoading problem. The overall goal of M-DAFTO is to
reduce the aggregate of∩¼éoading delay with increased assignment of
tasks to FNs. Extensive simulation and analysis con∩¼ürm a 30.26%
and a 93.53% reduction in of∩¼éoading delay and outages (unassigned
tasks) compared to the baselines.
Index Terms ΓÇöTask of∩¼éoading, IoT, fog systems, matching
theory, deferred acceptance algorithm, analytic hierarchy process,
technique for order performance by similarity to ideal solution.

Real-time services often require adequate computing resources
for processing such sizeable data. However, IoT devices are often
resource-constrained and incapable of performing large-scale
computations, relying on remote devices for processing [5].
Remote processing, often referred to as of∩¼éoading, is per-
formed in two modes depending on where the services are
executed. The ∩¼ürst execution mode directly of∩¼éoads the ser-
vices to a remote cloud server [6]. This mode of execution is
infeasible for real-time services as higher latency is experienced
due to physical distance , intermittent WAN latency , network
congestion, and scarce spectrum resources [5]. To overcome
the pitfalls, an alternate execution mode involves of∩¼éoading
computations to nearby fog nodes (FNs) instead of the remote
cloud server [1]. Additionally, of∩¼éoading to FNs provides real-
time mobility support, location awareness, and geo-distributed
services [7]. Although of∩¼éoading services to FNs is a sensible
option, generating an of∩¼éoading plan for a densely connected
IoT-Fog network with heterogeneous resources is non-trivial
and introduces multiple research challenges. First, the delay
guarantee of real-time services is dif∩¼ücult to achieve, especially
with the stochastic computational demands of IoTs and FNs.
Second, the contention of limited FN resources in a massive IoT
network often complicates the of∩¼éoading decisions. Finally, the
requirement of scalable, computationally-inexpensive, and non-
clustered assignment assists in effectively utilizing the limited
FN resources and avoiding congestion.
The existing works on of∩¼éoading focused on reducing the
of∩¼éoading delay or outages, i.e., services overshooting the pre-
scribed deadlines. In this regard, works in [4], [8], [9] adopted
meta-heuristic strategies to generate an of∩¼éoading schedule.
Though these approaches are acceptable, they are compute-
intensive, not scalable , and non-polynomial. Moreover, the
above-discussed approaches ignore the deadlines associated
with real-time services, thereby leading to quality-of-service
(QoS) violations [1]. The strategies discussed in [3], [10], [11],
[12] aimed to optimize the of∩¼éoading delay adhering to the QoS
requirements in terms of the deadline. However, they may suffer
from clustered assignments, resulting in ineffective utilization
and congestion at the FNs. Therefore, this work proposes a
framework M-DAFTO to simultaneously reduce of∩¼éoading de-
lays and outages. Adopting a multistage deferred acceptance
(MSDA) algorithm with maximum and minimum quotas at
the FNs assists in generating a polynomial time non-clustered
assignment.

II. R ELA TEDWORKS
This section discusses the relevant literature on of∩¼éoading
from two aspects. First, we discuss strategies that solely focus
on addressing the reduction in the of∩¼éoading delays. Reducing
the of∩¼éoading delay without any impetus on the maximum
tolerable latency of individual services may not be suitable for
real-world services such as AR and VR, with stringent QoS
expressed in terms of their deadlines[16]. For such applications,
QoS violations can result in user dissatisfaction and loss of
revenue for the service providers (SPs) in the long run, [15].
Therefore, the second part of the survey discusses strategies that
consider both the of∩¼éoading delay and outages while generating
an of∩¼éoading plan.
The works in [4], [8], [9] developed meta-heuristic-based
solutions to reduce theof∩¼éoading delayin complex fog networks.
In this regard, Kishor et al. [4] discussed an ef∩¼ücient of∩¼éoad-
ing policy based on a smart ant colony optimization (SACO).
Alternatively, a genetic algorithm (GA)ΓÇôbased swift of∩¼éoading
policy for sensors in an intelligent city network is discussed
in [8]. On the other hand, Wang and Chen [9] considered a setup
that considered both local and FN resources in making joint
of∩¼éoading decisions. A GA and simulated annealing (SA)ΓÇôbased
hybrid strategy is adopted to generate the of∩¼éoading schedule.
Some other works discussed in [17], [18], [22], and [23] have
also modeled of∩¼éoading as an optimization. Although the above-
discussed approaches generate acceptable solutions, they are
inef∩¼ücient, time-consuming, and not scalable [1]. To overcome
the lacunas of optimization solvers, authors in [13], [19], [20],
[21] proposed game-theoretic solutions. In this regard, Nowak
et al. [21] modeled the association between the access point and
cloudlets as a non-cooperative game. On the other hand, Chitti
et al. [13],a n dA l ie ta l . [20] formulated the of∩¼éoading as a
one-to-many matching game and solved it using a variation of the
DA algorithm. Alternatively, authors in[19] imposed maximum
and minimum quotas at the FNs to achieve a non-clustered as-
signment. They solved the matching problem using a multi-stage
deferred acceptance (MSDA) algorithm. Additionally, works
in [24], [25] focused on reducing theof∩¼éoading delayin different
environments.
As discussed earlier, a more realistic setup involves running
services within their speci∩¼üed deadlines. In this context, Za-
kariya et al. [10] discussed a GA-based solution that aims to
minimize the outages without compromising much with the of-
∩¼éoading latency. Deng et al. [11] proposed a novel hybrid meta-
heuristic scheme to execute maximum of∩¼éoaded tasks within
their respective deadlines to obtain the best of both worlds. The
authors hybridized GA and Particle swarm optimization (PSO)
to obtain a sub-optimal solution. Deviating from meta-heuristic-
based solutions, Adhikari et al. [3] proposed a delay-dependent
priority-aware heuristic of∩¼éoading strategy called DPTO. The
authors assign task priorities based on their deadlines and use
a multilevel feedback queue to schedule of∩¼éoaded tasks. Al-
ternatively, in [15], the authors formulated the of∩¼éoading as
a one-to-many matching-based with maximum and minimum
quotas at the FNs to achieve a non-clustered assignment. The
MSDA algorithm was adopted to solve the matching game,
which yielded a PL fair of∩¼éoading schedule. Some other works
on vehicular networks focused on of∩¼éoading latency reduction
with deadline speci∩¼ücations are discussed in [26], [27], [28].
It may be noted from the above discussion that of∩¼éoading
has been studied and investigated in the existing literature.

C. Task Of∩¼éoading as a Matching Game
The objective of M-DAFTO is to minimize total of∩¼éoading
delay. M-DAFTO achieves this objective through a matching
game between tasks and FNs, considering the preferences of
both agents.
Relationship of Task-FN Matching With M-DAFTOΓÇÖs Objec-
tive: M-DAFTO considers a many-to-one matching between
tasks (T ) and FNs (F), referred to as agents. Before assignment
(Algorithm 9), an agent ranks some/all agents in the opposite
set (Algorithm 2). For instance, tasks rank the FNs in increasing
order of of∩¼éoading delays. FNs order tasks in decreasing urgen-
cies (17), which considers the deadline, of∩¼éoading delay, and the
number of feasible execution execution options. The proposal

(14a). The of∩¼éoading delay and outages signi∩¼ücantly impact

logical to rank FNs in increasing order of of∩¼éoading delays.
Conversely, FNs prefer tasks with higher urgency to meet their

V. S OLUTION APPROACH
Traditionally, the deferred acceptance (DA) algorithm has
been successfully used to obtain a stable assignment in dif-
ferent scenarios [1], [20], [31]. However, the DA algorithm
fails to generate a matching that meets the minimum quota
of agents [30]. This is highlighted using an example presented
in Appendix B, available online. Therefore, in M-DAFTO,w e
utilize an improved version of the DA algorithm called the
MSDA algorithm that achieves a partially stable assignment.
Although the adoption of the MSDA algorithm meets the design
objective of M-DAFTO, it introduces the following additional
research challenges. First,i n M-DAFTO context, setting the
minimum quota is non-trivial owing to the heterogeneous nature
of FNs. Second, designing an agentΓÇÖs preferences is particularly
challenging as M-DAFTO caters to multiple parameters. Third,
determining the order of the tasks for processing is crucial
and more challenging because of their heterogeneous of∩¼éoading
requests. Subsequently, we discuss sophisticated procedures to
address each research challenge.

r A matching theory framework for tasks of∩¼éoading in fog

r LETO [15]: This work reduces the total of∩¼éoading delay

VII. C ONCLUSIONS &F UTURE DIRECTIONS
This paper proposed a framework M-DAFTO that generates
an ef∩¼ücient of∩¼éoading policy for the IoT-Fog interconnection
network. The of∩¼éoading is modeled as a matching game with
maximum and minimum quotas at the FNs. To overcome the
shortcomings of the DA and operate with minimum quotas,
we adopted the MSDA to solve the problem mentioned above.
Further, the MSDA generates a partial stable assignment be-
tween IoTs and FNs in a polynomial time. Simulation results
con∩¼ürm that the proposed scheme achieves 30.26 % and 93.53
% reduction of total of∩¼éoading delay and outages, respectively,
compared to the baselines. Though M-DAFTO achieves en-
hanced performance, the agentsΓÇÖ preferences are immutable.
The work can be extended to consider dynamic preferences,
including additional parameters such as of∩¼éoading energy and
channel interference. Moreover, the of∩¼éoading setup can also
be supplemented by including cloud resources in the IoT-Fog
interconnection network.
